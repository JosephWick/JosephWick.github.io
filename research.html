Astronomy & Astrophysics

I have worked with Professor Alexie Leauthaud for several years on various aspects of the Merian survey. Merian is a project designed to test SIDM by performing gravitational weak lensing measurements on dwarf galaxies. Currently I am working on a masters thesis analyzing hydrodynamic simulations of dwarf galaxies ran under SIDM and LCDM models to find differences that can be observed in H1.

Computational Seismology

I spent a summer as an intern at the Southern California Earthquake Center working with Dr. Val√®re Lambert to implment hierarchical matrices into simulations of earthquakes and aseismic slip (SEAS) that include viscoelastic effects.

The interplay between localized fault slip and off-fault viscoelastic deformation has substantial implications for the loading of stress on faults, the recurrence of earthquake ruptures, and the overall state of stress within the lithosphere. Analytic solutions relating stress and slip allow for efficient inclusion of bulk deformation in SEAS simulation using the integral method. However, in standard boundary integral method implementations, the number of calculations necessary scales quadratically with the number of computational elements, limiting the feasibility of larger scale simulations.
