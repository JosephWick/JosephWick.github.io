About Me

In October 2023, I began a position in the CPAC group at Argonne National Lab. I work closely with Dr. Matthew Becker in the development of a pipeline of differentiable predictions of cosmological summary statistics for use on Argonne's large computing resources. I completed my masters in Scientific Computing and Applied Mathematics (SciCAM) at UCSC in the summer of 2023. As part of the SciCAM program, I am wrote my thesis on analyzing hydrodynamical simulations for evidence of feedback driven breathing modes in dwarf galaxies. I compared these predictions to spectroscopic data that I helped to take using the Keck telescope.

Prior to my graduate education, I studied physics and computer science at the undergraduate level, graduating in 2022. As an undergraduate, I worked on some data aspects of the Merian survey with Professor Leauthaud, and observed remotely on the Keck and Blanco telescopes. I also spent a summer as an intern at the Southern California Earthquake Center, where I implemented hierarchical matrices into a simulation of earthquakes and aseismic slip (SEAS), and attended the annual SCEC and Seismological Society of America conferences.

I first became involved in applied math and computational physics through an interest in software development, and I still enjoy working on small personal projects. When I'm not busy applying the math or furiously typing, I also enjoy the outside world. I am a fan of both watching and playing soccer, riding bikes, and I recently learned how to sew.
